Generalization to higher dimensions, rough intuition:

Minkowski ℝ1,𝑛 can be decomposed into a 1 dim distance space ℝ≥0 and an 𝑛 dim direction space {⟨𝑥⟩2=±1}. Let's use their product topology.

Now we need to define the topology of the direction space ℚ1,𝑛(±1). We can use the 𝑛 dim geodesic coordinates of ℚ1,𝑛(±1). We can use the topology of geodesic coordinates, which is of ℝ𝑛 type or ℝ1,𝑛−1 type. By induction on dimension, we need to define the topology of ℝ1,1, which again decomposes into a 1 dim radial space and a 1 dim direction space ℚ1,1. Now the 1 dim direction space is just a hyperbolic curve, which is a Riemann submanifold, metric inherited from the metric of ℝ1,1.

To define geodesics using pure quadratic form techniques, a geodesic starting from 𝑝∈ℚ1,𝑛(±1) is an embedded hyperbola ℍ𝕪, defined as ℚ1,𝑛(±1) and the 2 dim space spanned by 𝑝 and 𝑞∈𝑝⟂.

The tangent space of the quadratic manifold ℚ𝑝,𝑞(±1) can be defined as the (affine) subspace orthogonal to the radial direction
